Where Bitly is the better pick

Bitly is excellent at what it was built for. If this is your world, it's the right tool.

Short links at scale. Bitly is the category standard for branded short links and custom back-halves. If shortening and tracking links is your primary channel, Bitly does it deeper than any QR platform.

Link infrastructure. Domain tools like Domain Guard, a 404 fixer, and root routing, plus link-in-bio and a UTM builder, make Bitly a complete link-management system.

A known short domain. A bit.ly link is a recognized trust signal. For pure link sharing, that recognition has real value.

If links are your main channel and QR Codes are occasional, Bitly is the simpler fit. The rest of this page is for teams whose primary channel is QR.

FAQ — Uniqode vs. Bitly

Can I move my Bitly QR Codes to Uniqode?
Yes, if your QR Codes use a custom domain. Those move to Uniqode and keep working as printed. QR Codes on Bitly's own short domain can't move, so you can keep running them on Bitly while you build new campaigns on Uniqode.
Will I lose my scan history when I switch?
Export your Bitly data before your plan lapses. Bitly holds scan history for 30 days to 2 years depending on plan, so the export is your permanent copy. Uniqode starts tracking each code fresh from the day it moves over.
Do I have to reprint anything?
Not if your QR Codes use a custom domain. Codes already printed on signage, packaging, or collateral keep working after the move. Codes on Bitly's short domain stop working when your plan ends, so plan new prints for those.
Can Uniqode shorten links too?
Yes. Uniqode creates short links, including in bulk, alongside QR Codes, so a team moving off Bitly can keep both in one place. Uniqode's depth is in QR; if short links are your only need, see the section above on where Bitly fits best.
Is Bitly good for QR Codes?
Bitly makes dynamic URL QR Codes with click tracking, which works for simple link-to-website use. It caps QR Codes per month (2 to 200 by plan) and supports URL codes only, so for QR-led campaigns or multiple QR types, it runs out of room quickly.
Which is better for QR-led marketing campaigns?
Uniqode. It treats QR as the primary channel: hundreds of active codes, every QR type, Smart Rules routing, GPS-level analytics, and retargeting. Bitly treats QR as an add-on to link management, metered by the month.
Which is better for short links?
Bitly. Branded short links, custom back-halves, and domain tools are its core product, and a bit.ly link is a recognized trust signal. Uniqode shortens links too, but link management is Bitly's home ground.
How many QR Codes does Bitly allow?
Bitly allows 2 QR Codes a month free, 5 at $10, 10 at $29, and 200 at $199, counted per month. Uniqode includes 15, 250, 500, or 2,000 active codes by plan, which you can edit and reuse rather than recreate monthly.
Is Uniqode cheaper than Bitly for QR Codes?
For real QR volume, yes. Bitly's $199 plan caps QR at 200 a month; Uniqode's $99 plan includes 500 active codes. The more QR Codes you run, the more Uniqode wins on both price and capacity.
How much do Uniqode and Bitly cost?
Uniqode publishes plans at $9, $49, $99, and $399 a month plus Enterprise. Bitly publishes Free, Core $10, Growth $29, Premium $199, plus Enterprise. Bitly is cheaper at entry; Uniqode gives far more QR capacity at each step up.
What QR Code types does Bitly support?
Bitly supports dynamic URL QR Codes only. Uniqode adds vCard, Wi-Fi, PDF, app download, social, forms, Linkpages, geolocation, and multi-language QR Codes.
Does Bitly have Smart Rules or conditional routing?
No. Bitly QR Codes point to one destination. Uniqode's Smart Rules route a single code by time, location, device, device language, or scan count, with the conditions combinable.
Do Uniqode and Bitly track scan locations?
Both track scans. Uniqode shows GPS-level locations from $49 when scanners allow it, with city, state, and country otherwise. Bitly's city-level data starts at its $199 Premium plan.
Can QR Codes be password protected?
Uniqode password-protects any QR Code. Bitly lists no password protection for QR Codes.
